Problem Solving Techniques and Processes

  • Pseudocode – Writing out code in plain English, before you write your code
  • Rubber ducky method – Don’t understand, talk to a rubber duck, a cat or someone else. Often saying it out loud allows you to understand it better
  • Reading error messages – Don’t understand the message, google it or keywords in the error to find a solution
  • Console log – One of JavaScript’s most common approaches to troubleshoot is to log stuff utilizing console.log(); I don’t use this enough, but if it works for you go for it. console.log();
  • Googling – My number one choice. Don’t know “google it”
  • Asking your peers for help – Great option when you know the grasp on a topic is better than your own
  • Asking coaches for help – Ask and you shall receive. They are a wealth of knowledge
  • Improving your process with reflection – My worst of the list, I need to take more notes
